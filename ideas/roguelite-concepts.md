# Roguelite Game Concepts

Here are a few creative directions for a **roguelite** game that step outside the typical fighting/deck-building/hack'n'slash genres. They maintain the "choose 1 of 3 upgrades" mid-run mechanic and feature meta-progression (permanent upgrades that persist after each run's death/failure).

---

## 1. Corporate Ladder ("Office Politics")

You play as an entry-level worker trying to survive the chaotic workday and climb to the top floor (the CEO's office) before getting fired or burning out.

*   **Gameplay Core:** Resource management and social stealth. You manage "Stress", "Productivity", and "Influence".
*   **Run Upgrades (Performance Reviews):**
    *   *BCC Everyone:* An AoE move that deflects blame to other departments.
    *   *Double Espresso:* Temporarily increases action speed but adds a trailing stress debuff.
    *   *Synergy:* Passively combos your current active tasks for a multiplier on productivity.
*   **Permanent Upgrades (Salary / Networking):**
    *   *Nepotism Perk:* Start with an automatic pass on the first "blunder".
    *   *Better Desk Chair:* Max Stress capacity is permanently increased.
    *   *Middle Management Start:* Skip the first 10 floors of the tower.
    *   **(Meta-Currency: "Severance Pay" earned from failed runs)**

## 2. Botany & Biomes ("Deep Roots")

You play as an ancient seed slowly growing into a massive tree, trying to survive shifting seasons, extreme weather, and invasive pests.

*   **Gameplay Core:** Spatial puzzle and expansion. You choose where to spread roots for nutrients and where to grow branches for sunlight.
*   **Run Upgrades (Evolutionary Traits):**
    *   *Mycorrhizal Network:* Allows sharing of resources with neighboring plants to create a defensive perimeter.
    *   *Thick Bark:* Resist damage from frost and woodpeckers.
    *   *Toxic Sap:* Damages insects that try to feed on your leaves.
*   **Permanent Upgrades (Genetic Memory):**
    *   *Hybrid Seeds:* Start future runs as a different tree species (e.g., Oak for defense, Willow for flexibility).
    *   *Rich Soil:* Start with a pool of stored nutrients.
    *   *Climate Adaptation:* Permanent resistance to the first specific weather event of the run.
    *   **(Meta-Currency: "Compost/Fertilizer" earned from previous dead trees)**

## 3. The Culinary Rush ("Kitchen Chaos")

You are the head chef trying to survive the busiest service night of the year without the restaurant shutting down.

*   **Gameplay Core:** Time-management and multitasking. Orders come in fast, and you have to string together cooking processes efficiently.
*   **Run Upgrades (Mid-Shift Adaptations):**
    *   *Sous-Chef Shout:* Instantly complete all prep work for the current tickets.
    *   *Teflon Coating:* Food cannot burn for the next 60 seconds.
    *   *Menu Substitution:* Swap out a highly complex ticket for a simpler one without losing reputation.
*   **Permanent Upgrades (Restaurant Renovation):**
    *   *Sharper Knives:* Base prep speed is permanently increased by 10%.
    *   *Premium Suppliers:* Ingredients have a chance to instantly become "perfect" quality.
    *   *Expanded Stovetop:* Unlock an extra burner to handle more simultaneous dishes.
    *   **(Meta-Currency: "Tips/Reviews" earned from the service)**

## 4. Cosmic Architect ("Gravitational Pull")

You are a sentient gravitational anomaly trying to build a stable solar system while fending off rogue asteroids, supernovas, and black holes.

*   **Gameplay Core:** Physics-based simulation and balancing. You add mass, adjust orbits, and try to keep your planets in the "Goldilocks zone".
*   **Run Upgrades (Cosmic Events):**
    *   *Tidal Locking:* Completely stabilizes the orbit of one chosen planet.
    *   *Gas Giant Lure:* Spawns a massive planet that attracts incoming rogue asteroids away from your fragile inner worlds.
    *   *Solar Flare:* A burst of energy from your star that obliterates incoming threats in a specific sector.
*   **Permanent Upgrades (Stardust Accumulation):**
    *   *Binary Star System:* Start with two stars, completely changing the orbital physics and energy pools.
    *   *Dense Core:* Your central star has a stronger baseline gravitational pull.
    *   *Primordial Soup:* Planets have a higher chance to generate life, which provides unique late-game bonuses.
    *   **(Meta-Currency: "Dark Matter" collected during cosmic collapses)**
